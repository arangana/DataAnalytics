Stanton's Professional Background

Tony's career in graphic media and education spans three decades. After graduating from Colorado College in 1972, Tony worked for four years before enrolling in graduate school at the University of Maryland where he received a Master of Industrial Education degree in Graphic Arts Education. After teaching briefly at the U. of Md., Tony went on to Rochester Institute of Technology where he earned a Master of Science Degree in Printing Technology with emphasis on color reproduction. Tony then taught for the California State University system for three years before taking a job in research with the Graphic Arts Technical Foundation and obtaining a Ph.D. in Instructional Technology from the University of Pittsburgh. Tony spent 12 years at GATF designing process control devices for printing and doing research on process characterization and control. In 1996, he took his current position as Director of Graphic Media Management at the Tepper School of Business at Carnegie Mellon University.

Education

  • The Colorado College - B.A. - 1972
  • University of Maryland - M.Ed. - 1979
  • Rochester Institute of Technology - M.S. - 1982
  • University of Pittsburgh - Ph.D. - 1992

Teaching Interests

Graphic media technology with emphasis on emerging technologies and color calibration and control. Current research activities include test devices for digital systems and attributes for characterization of visual phenomenon.
